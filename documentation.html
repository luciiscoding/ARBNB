Horesca Traveling: Integrating Mobile and Mixed Reality Technologies for Enhanced Accommodation Services

Bendoiu Laura, Bujenita Lucian, Hogea Ana | "Alexandru Ioan Cuza" University, Department of Computer Science

Journal of Hospitality and Tourism Technology Innovation
May 11, 2025
Abstract: This paper examines the conceptual framework and system architecture of Horesca Traveling, an innovative digital platform that combines mobile application technology with Mixed Reality (MR) capabilities to enhance the accommodation discovery and booking experience. Building on recent research that demonstrates how augmented reality significantly enhances tourism experiences and increases user satisfaction (Onirix, 2023; RipenApps, 2023), the platform uniquely integrates conventional property listings with immersive virtual property exploration, augmented reality on-site assistance, and comprehensive accommodation analytics. Drawing on systematic reviews of virtual reality applications in tourism (ScienceDirect, 2023), this study analyzes the user interface design principles, technological integration patterns, and potential impact on user decision-making processes in the hospitality sector, while also considering implementation challenges and future development trajectories in light of emerging research on customer experience dimensions in sharing economy accommodations.
Keywords: Mixed Reality, Hospitality Technology, Mobile Applications, Augmented Reality, User Experience Design, Travel Technology, Accommodation Services, Guest Satisfaction, Memorability

1. Introduction

The digital transformation of the hospitality industry has accelerated dramatically in recent years, with platforms such as Airbnb and Booking.com revolutionizing how travelers discover, evaluate, and reserve accommodations (Guttentag, 2019). However, research indicates that traditional mobile applications often fail to provide immersive experiences that accurately represent properties or offer intuitive on-site guidance, leading to potential gaps between guest expectations and reality (Pushti Ranpura, 2023). Horesca Traveling represents a novel approach to these limitations by creating an ecosystem that spans both mobile device functionality and Mixed Reality (MR) experiences, addressing the increasing consumer demand for enhanced pre-booking visualization and on-site support identified in recent studies (Onirix, 2023; RipenApps, 2023).

This paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the Horesca Traveling platform, examining its core components, technological underpinnings, and potential implications for the hospitality industry. Recent systematic reviews of 54 papers on virtual reality applications in tourism (ScienceDirect, 2023) highlight the growing importance of immersive technologies in enhancing tourist experiences. By integrating conventional mobile application features with cutting-edge MR capabilities, Horesca Traveling aims to address critical pain points in the traveler journey while providing enhanced transparency and utility throughout the accommodation selection process. This approach aligns with emerging research on mobile augmented reality travel guides, which demonstrates significant improvements in user experience through personalized recommendations and contextual information delivery (ScienceDirect, 2014).

2. System Architecture and Components

Horesca Traveling consists of two primary technological components working in tandem to create a seamless user experience:

2.1 Mobile Application

The mobile application serves as the primary interface for account management, property discovery, and transaction processing. Analysis of the application interface reveals four primary functional modules:

2.1.1 User Authentication and Profile Management

The system employs a comprehensive user management framework with distinct pathways for account creation, authentication, and profile customization. The login interface supports traditional credential-based authentication as well as social authentication options via Google and Apple identity services. The profile management interface allows users to maintain personal information, preferences, and historical data, enabling personalized recommendations and streamlined booking experiences.

2.1.2 Property Discovery and Navigation

The property discovery module presents accommodations in a visually-dominant interface that prioritizes photography and key data points including price (displayed in RON currency), rating scores, and accommodation classifications. The interface employs horizontal scrolling galleries with categorization options for filtering properties based on location, style, budget, specific amenities, and trending properties. This design pattern aligns with contemporary mobile interface conventions while optimizing for scannable information architecture.

2.1.3 Favorites and Collection Management

The favorites module enables users to curate personalized collections of preferred properties, supporting decision-making processes that typically span multiple sessions. Each saved property displays consistent metadata including pricing, rating, and property classification, facilitating efficient comparison and selection.

2.1.4 Property Statistics and Analytics

Distinguished from conventional booking applications, Horesca Traveling provides in-depth property analytics and detailed descriptions. The statistics module offers comprehensive property information including multi-dimensional rating systems, pricing analyses, and detailed descriptions in the user's preferred language. This represents a significant enhancement over traditional booking platforms by centralizing decision-critical information and contextual details.

2.2 Mixed Reality (MR) Application

The Mixed Reality component of Horesca Traveling extends the platform's functionality into immersive and augmented environments, offering two distinct experiential modes that align with recent research on augmented reality applications in tourism (Onirix, 2023; RipenApps, 2023):

2.2.1 Remote Property Exploration

Utilizing MR headset technology, users can virtually navigate potential accommodations prior to physical arrival. This capability transcends traditional photography by enabling spatial understanding, scale appreciation, and interactive exploration of property features. Systematic reviews of virtual reality in tourism have demonstrated that such immersive pre-visit experiences significantly enhance decision confidence and reduce potential dissatisfaction caused by expectation-reality gaps (ScienceDirect, 2023). This functionality addresses a significant limitation in traditional booking platforms—the inability to accurately assess spatial relationships and environmental qualities through static imagery alone—which research has identified as a key factor in guest dissatisfaction (ScienceDirect, 2023).

2.2.2 On-Site Augmented Assistance

Once at the property, the MR component transitions to an augmented reality mode, overlaying practical information within the physical environment. This includes location-specific guidance (e.g., key storage locations), appliance operation instructions, and contextual information about property features. Research on mobile augmented reality travel guides such as CorfuAR has demonstrated that contextual, personalized information delivery significantly enhances tourist experiences by providing relevant information at the moment of need (ScienceDirect, 2014). This functionality reduces cognitive load during the check-in process and enhances the overall user experience by eliminating common friction points associated with unfamiliar environments, directly addressing key dissatisfaction factors identified in comparative studies of Airbnb and hotel experiences (ScienceDirect, 2023).

3. User Interface Analysis

Examination of the Horesca Traveling interface reveals several noteworthy design patterns and principles:

3.1 Visual Hierarchy and Information Architecture

The interface employs a clear visual hierarchy that prioritizes imagery for emotional engagement while structuring practical information in consistent, scannable formats. Price points are prominently displayed with high contrast, while ratings utilize recognizable iconography to enable rapid quality assessment. The navigation system employs both bottom tab navigation for primary functions and contextual filtering options for content refinement.

3.2 Cross-Platform Consistency

Visual and functional consistency between the mobile and MR interfaces creates a cohesive ecosystem that reduces learning curves and cognitive friction when transitioning between devices. This design approach reflects contemporary best practices in cross-platform experience design, particularly important given the emerging nature of MR technologies in consumer applications.

3.3 Multi-Modal Interaction Patterns

The system accommodates diverse interaction modalities including touch-based manipulation (mobile), spatial gestures (MR environment), and potentially voice commands (implied by the contextual assistance functionality). This flexibility acknowledges the different contexts in which users engage with the platform throughout the traveler journey.

4. Technological Considerations and Implementation Challenges

The implementation of a platform like Horesca Traveling presents several technical challenges and considerations:

4.1 Data Integration and Synchronization

Seamless operation across mobile and MR environments requires sophisticated data synchronization mechanisms to ensure consistency between devices and contexts. Property information, user preferences, and saved collections must maintain integrity across multiple endpoints, necessitating robust API architecture and state management protocols.

4.2 Mixed Reality Content Creation and Maintenance

The creation of accurate, useful, and up-to-date MR experiences for diverse properties presents significant content production challenges. Each property requires individual spatial mapping, information tagging, and ongoing maintenance to ensure virtual representations match current physical conditions. This represents a substantial operational overhead compared to traditional photography-based listings.

4.3 Device Accessibility and Market Penetration

While mobile devices enjoy near-universal adoption, MR headsets remain relatively specialized consumer products with limited market penetration. This dichotomy creates implementation challenges, as the platform must provide sufficient value through its mobile component while incentivizing adoption of the MR capabilities. A potential strategy involves offering enhanced features through MR while ensuring core functionality remains accessible via the mobile application alone.

5. Implications for Hospitality Industry and Consumer Behavior

The introduction of platforms like Horesca Traveling may catalyze several shifts in both industry practices and consumer behavior, as suggested by current research:

5.1 Enhanced Transparency and Reduced Information Asymmetry

By enabling immersive property exploration and providing comprehensive analytics, Horesca Traveling potentially reduces information asymmetry between property managers and prospective guests. Research analyzing sentiment in reviews across different platforms (arXiv) has identified significant differences in review positivity between sharing economy platforms like Airbnb and traditional booking platforms, suggesting potential reliability issues in user-generated content. The objective visualization capabilities of Horesca Traveling address this concern by providing unfiltered property representations. This increased transparency may drive quality improvements within the market as properties unable to withstand enhanced scrutiny face competitive disadvantages, aligning with research findings indicating declining satisfaction trends in accommodation reviews over time (Taylor & Francis, 2022).

5.2 Shift in Decision-Making Processes

Traditional accommodation selection typically follows a funnel model where users progressively narrow options based on increasingly detailed evaluation. Recent research on Airbnb guest preferences has identified distinct differences in decision factors between guests seeking entire properties versus shared accommodations, with the former prioritizing hedonic value and the latter focusing on utilitarian aspects (Frontiers in Psychology, 2023). The immersive capabilities of Horesca Traveling may reshape this process by enabling deeper evaluation of both hedonic and utilitarian factors earlier in the decision journey, potentially reducing consideration sets more rapidly and increasing conversion rates from discovery to booking.

5.3 New Property Differentiation Strategies

As properties become more transparently comparable through immersive technologies, differentiation strategies may evolve beyond photography and written descriptions. Research examining Airbnb experience dimensions has identified four key factors that significantly influence customer loyalty: home benefits, personalized services, authenticity, and social connection (Emerald Insight, 2018). Properties may increasingly design spaces with "virtual visit appeal" in mind and invest in creating distinctive augmented reality experiences that highlight these experiential dimensions as competitive advantages. Furthermore, research on memorable Airbnb experiences (Emerald Insight, 2022) suggests that properties might leverage MR technology to emphasize aspects that drive memorability, such as authentic local experiences and unique home attributes, thereby enhancing platform attachment and loyalty.

6. Future Development Trajectories

Several potential development paths could further enhance the Horesca Traveling concept:

6.1 Integration of Artificial Intelligence

AI algorithms could enhance the platform by providing personalized property recommendations, predictive pricing analysis, and automated creation of MR content from standard photography and floor plans. Natural language processing could further augment the on-site assistance features through conversational interactions about property features and local information.

6.2 Social and Collaborative Features

Future iterations might incorporate collaborative decision-making tools that allow travel groups to collectively explore and evaluate properties in shared virtual spaces. Social features could enable travelers to share annotated virtual experiences with their networks, creating organic marketing opportunities for exceptional properties.

6.3 Expanded Reality Spectrum Implementation

While the current concept employs mixed reality, future development could explore implementations across the full reality-virtuality continuum—from fully virtual exploratory environments to minimally augmented on-site experiences, with appropriate technological deployments for each use case.

7. Conclusion

Horesca Traveling represents a significant conceptual advancement in accommodation service platforms by integrating conventional mobile functionality with immersive mixed reality experiences. This approach addresses longstanding limitations in traditional booking platforms while creating new possibilities for property exploration, evaluation, and on-site assistance.

By providing travelers with enhanced visualization capabilities, comprehensive analytics, and contextual on-site guidance, Horesca Traveling potentially reduces decision uncertainty while enhancing the overall accommodation experience. However, implementation challenges related to content creation, device accessibility, and operational scalability present substantial hurdles to widespread adoption.

As mixed reality technologies become increasingly mainstream, concepts like Horesca Traveling are likely to influence consumer expectations regarding pre-purchase evaluation and on-site service delivery across the hospitality industry. Future research should examine actual implementation outcomes, user adoption patterns, and impacts on property booking conversion rates to fully assess the practical value of such integrated approaches.
